## 2026-07-22

- **Build-contract hardening (#737)**: `.claude/skills/build-catalog-component/CONVENTIONS.md` gained two new sections — `## Reading rendered artifacts` (a bounded-window reading discipline binding any artifact over 500 lines by **size, not provenance**, so a vendored `manifests/**` CRD file is in scope exactly as a render is: inventory via `^kind:`, then lens-bounded windows, with any entry the agent neither opened nor judged named in its output; an artifact absent at its explicit path is regenerated by any Bash-holding agent, `catalog-evaluator` included, and only a read-only reviewer records it as not verifiable in this dispatch, through the field its own schema already carries — `not_locally_verifiable` where there is one, `notes:` where there is not — with `verdict: needs-info` only when its judgment depended on that artifact; every route uses vocabulary an existing agent contract defines, and none invents a severity, an enum value, or a verdict state) and `## Render-grounded claims` (a claim about rendered output or chart behaviour is written only after being read from the render or the chart template, binding the builder's own work, every orchestrator-authored brief, and every render claim the orchestrator itself cites). In `.claude/skills/build-catalog-component/SKILL.md` the two disciplines have deliberately different reach: the **reading discipline** is brief content at **five** verify-and-review dispatch sites — the Phase-3 evaluator, the Phase-4 fixer, the Phase-5 reviewers, the Phase-5 semantic-bearing-fix implementer re-dispatch, and the Phase-6 step-2 evaluator re-dispatch — so none of those briefs depends on the orchestrator remembering it. The Phase-2 build dispatch is deliberately not among them: it reads `CONVENTIONS.md` whole and inherits the rule from the file itself. The **grounding rule** as `CONVENTIONS.md` states it binds every brief the orchestrator authors *and* every render claim the orchestrator itself cites; `SKILL.md` spells that obligation out at the **two orchestrator-authored implementer briefs** (Phase-4 fixer, Phase-5 semantic-bearing-fix re-dispatch), which are the sites where an ungrounded claim has historically become an instruction. Re-verified `workflows/catalog-build-pipeline.md` and `decisions/DR-0001-specification-driven-component-build.md` (both list the edited `CONVENTIONS.md` in `sources:`): purely additive, no present-tense claim falsified; timestamps bumped.
